Igor Laltuf began his studies in economics at Fluminense Federal University in 2009, simultaneously participating in the meetings of the Macroeconomic Environment Group and working at the junior enterprise.  Later, as an undergraduate, he started working as assistant researcher on an interinstitutional research project on the National Bank for Economic and Social Development (BNDES), dominant class fractions and their influence on capital accumulation in Brazil. The project was funded by the Ford Foundation, coordinated by the State, Labor, Territory and Nature Laboratory (ETTERN-IPPUR-UFRJ), involving colleagues from UFF, UFRJ , UFRRJ and UNIR. After completing his monograph on the environmental implications of BNDES financing, which was presented to a class with seventy students and approved with praise, he was invited to present the research again to classes in the discipline of economics research methodology and technique. In the following years, he continued working on the BNDES research project, which together with the work of the group's researchers resulted in the publication of the book BNDES: economic groups, public sector and civil society in 2017. 

Seeking to develop an interdisciplinary vision and research more pragmatic social issues, in 2019 he joined the master's course in Urban and Regional Research and Planning and is researching investments in urban mobility infrastructure in the city of Rio de Janeiro. Also participates in the City, Law and Mobility Research Group (CiDiMob / IPPUR / UFRJ) and is member of the ATEMAB (Technical and Educational Advisory on the Environment and Dams) team responsible for preparing the damage matrix resulting from triggering the Emergency Action Plan for Mining Dams (PAEBM) in Itatiaiuçu/MG.

Research Interests

  • Urban mobility
  • Sustainable development
